About Me

Past

My early education focused on the arts and humanities: I chose Art, English Language and Literature, and Classical Civilisations at A-level, and studied History of Art and Philosophy at UCL for my undergraduate degree. I've always been naturally creative, and continue to draw, paint, and take photos in my spare time.

After graduating I went to work for the Tate galleries in Visitor Experience, and somehow also fell into event management, overseeing exhibitions, alternative parties, and festivals. The unifying feature of my various endeavours is a passion for communicating with people.

I've decided to change tack and try something new, and web development seemed like a natural way to combine creativity with a drive for effective communication, whilst also challenging me to learn new languages and skills. Indeed, one of the things that drew me to programming is that it’s constantly evolving and changing, requiring constant learning.
